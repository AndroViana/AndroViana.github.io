Padre Burgos Day

Padre Burgos, Quezon, is known for its vibrant street dance and grand parade during its annual town fiesta. The colorful festivities showcase the town's culture, history, and traditions. Locals and visitors alike can enjoy the lively and exciting performances by dance groups, marching bands, and parade floats. The event culminates with a thanksgiving mass, giving thanks for the blessings received throughout the year.

Karera Ng Sibid

"Karera ng Sibid" is a swimming competition held in Padre Burgos, Quezon. The competition features the "sibid," a traditional wooden board used for fishing, as the only means of propelling oneself across a 60-meter distance. Participants showcase their skills and endurance in the race, which has become a unique and exciting event for locals and visitors alike. The event not only highlights the town's cultural heritage but also promotes physical fitness and community spirit.

Pagoda

The Pagoda of Padre Burgos in Quezon is a symbol of the town's strong Catholic faith and devotion to the Virgin Mary. It is a colorful and intricately-designed boat-shaped structure used during the town's annual fluvial procession in honor of Our Lady of the Most Holy Rosary. The Pagoda is adorned with flowers, banners, and lights, and it is a highlight of the celebration that draws thousands of devotees and tourists every year.

Laguimanok Festival

The Padre Burgos town in Quezon Province, Philippines celebrates the Laguimanok Festival every 15th of May. The festival features various activities such as street dancing, a parade, and a cook-off competition of the town's specialty dish, "Laguimanok" or chicken cooked in coconut milk. The festival highlights the town's rich culture and culinary heritage.

Municipality Of Padre Burgos

Padre Burgos is a fourth-class municipality in the province of Quezon, Philippines. It is known for its beautiful beaches, lush forests, and delicious local cuisine.
